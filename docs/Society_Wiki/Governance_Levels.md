# Group Size and Governance Hierarchy

This framework balances participation, speed, and accountability.  
It assumes the principle: **keep decisions as local as possible, federate only when needed**.

## Level 1: Micro Group (1-5 members)

- Structure: 1 coordinator (or rotating facilitator), no formal council required.
- Decision style: consensus or simple majority.
- Membership: all members can approve invites together.
- Fragmentation: not needed.

## Level 2: Small Circle (6-20 members)

- Structure: 1 elected leader + 1 deputy/secretary.
- Decision style: majority for normal decisions; higher threshold for rule changes.
- Membership: circle-level admissions process (not leader-only).
- Fragmentation trigger: when active membership nears 30, prepare split plan.

## Level 3: Growth Circle (21-30 members)

- Structure: 1 leader + 2-3 role leads (operations, membership, finance/moderation).
- Decision style: local assembly + delegated operational decisions.
- Membership: dedicated admissions sub-circle recommended.
- Fragmentation: **soft trigger active**. Start designing two daughter circles.

## Level 4: Pre-Federation (31-40 members)

- Structure: leadership pair plus mini-council (3-5).
- Decision style: clear split between local decisions and cross-group matters.
- Membership: formal process with published criteria and appeals.
- Fragmentation: **hard trigger zone**. Group should split into 2 circles by 40.

## Level 5: Federated Community (41-100+ members)

- Structure: multiple circles (target 12-30 each), each with elected local leader.
- Coordination: federation council (circle leaders + rotating citizen delegates).
- Decision style:
  - Local circle decides local matters.
  - Federation council decides shared infrastructure and inter-circle issues.
  - Full-community vote for constitutional changes and major rights/resource shifts.
- Fragmentation rule: any circle that exceeds 35 active members enters split workflow.

## When to Fragment the Group

Use two thresholds:

- **Soft split trigger: 30 active members** in one circle  
  Start planning split boundaries, leadership elections, and role transfer.

- **Hard split trigger: 40 active members** in one circle  
  Split becomes mandatory within a defined window (for example, 30 days).

Rationale:

- Around 30+, participation quality and trust density usually decline.
- Around 40+, coordination overhead rises sharply and leader bottlenecks appear.

## Default Split Workflow

1. Announce split review when circle reaches 30.
2. Draft two new circle maps (membership + functional scope).
3. Members vote on split proposal.
4. Elect leaders for each new circle.
5. Move shared issues to federation council.
6. Run 60-day post-split audit and rebalance if needed.

## Minimum Safeguards at All Levels

- Fixed terms and recall for leaders.
- Transparent decision logs.
- Conflict-of-interest declarations.
- Appeals process for membership and sanctions.
- Periodic audit of finance, process, and rights integrity.
